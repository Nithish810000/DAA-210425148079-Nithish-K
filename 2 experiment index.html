02 / THE METHODS

Three routes to
the same answer.

No single method wins everywhere. Their trade-offs emerge from what they remember, what they skip, and what they calculate.

01DIRECT

Naive Search

Align the pattern at every possible position and compare left to right. Simple, transparent, and surprisingly effective on small inputs.

Worst time
O(nm)
Preprocessing
None
Best for
Short text
02PREFIX

KMP

Use the longest-prefix-suffix table to avoid rechecking characters already known to match. Predictable linear performance.

Worst time
O(n+m)
Preprocessing
O(m)
Best for
Repeated patterns
03HASH
#101

Rabin–Karp

Compare rolling hashes first, then verify characters only when hashes agree. A natural fit for searching many patterns.

Average time
O(n+m)
Worst time
O(nm)
Best for
Multi-pattern
